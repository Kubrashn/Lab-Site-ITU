Acoustic Sensing and Imaging Laboratory (AKAL)

What is AKAL

The main goal of Acoustic Sensing and Imaging Laboratory is to conduct research on new topics mostly about acoustics and signal processing and aid the ongoing activities in ARIS LAB.

Our Projects

  1. Recording and Analysis of Bats Activity on the University Campus using High-Frequency Microphones and Thermal Cameras in Arrays
  2. Recording and Analysis Construction Ruins after a Natural Disaster Aimed at Detecting Signs of Life using Geophone Arrays
  3. Recording and Analysis of Underwater Activities at Sea Ports using Hydrophone Arrays
  4. Synthesis and Imaging of Tissue Mimicking Materials (a.k.a. Phantoms)
  5. Producing Heatmaps of Classrooms and Corridors in the Electronics and Electrical Engineering Faculty Building using Thermal Cameras
  6. Generating Noise Map of the University Campus using High-Frequency Microphones
  7. Applying Various Image/Signal Processing Techniques on Recorded Footage via High-Speed, Night-Vision, and Thermal Cameras
